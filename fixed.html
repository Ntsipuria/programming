Fixed Bridge

Fixed bridges are immovable structures providing a continuous pathway over obstacles, and they are the most common type of bridge.

Suitability

Ideal for areas without navigational clearance requirements.

Suitable for low-maintenance needs.

Effective in stable terrains with predictable load requirements.

Pros and Cons

Pros

Durable and low-maintenance.

Can handle high traffic and heavy loads.

Aesthetically versatile.

Cons

Unsuitable for waterways with tall ships.

Requires extensive initial construction.

Examples

Pont Neuf, Paris, France: A historic stone fixed bridge.

Confederation Bridge, Canada: A fixed link over water.

Chesapeake Bay Bridge, USA: A fixed bridge crossing a bay.
