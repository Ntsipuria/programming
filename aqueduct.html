Aqueduct Bridge

Aqueducts are bridges designed to transport water across gaps, valleys, or uneven terrain.

Suitability

Areas needing water transportation over obstacles.

Effective in arid regions for irrigation.

Suitable for historic preservation or aesthetic value.

Pros and Cons

Pros

Efficient water transportation.

Historic and cultural significance.

Low environmental impact.

Cons

Limited modern use.

High construction and restoration costs.

Examples

Pont du Gard, France: A Roman aqueduct and UNESCO World Heritage site.

Aqueduct of Segovia, Spain: A historic Roman aqueduct.

Veluwemeer Aqueduct, Netherlands: A modern waterway crossing over a road.
