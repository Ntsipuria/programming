Floating Bridge

Floating bridges are supported by pontoons or boats, allowing them to float on the water surface. They are anchored to stabilize the structure.

Suitability

Deep water or soft beds where traditional piers are infeasible.

Temporary or emergency uses.

Suitable for areas requiring flexible solutions.

Pros and Cons

Pros

Quick to construct.

Minimal impact on the waterbed.

Cost-effective for temporary spans.

Cons

Limited durability.

Vulnerable to extreme weather.

Restricted load capacity.

Examples

Evergreen Point Floating Bridge, USA: The world’s longest floating bridge.

Lacey V. Murrow Memorial Bridge, USA: A floating bridge carrying Interstate 90.

Höga Kusten Bridge, Sweden: Includes floating sections.
