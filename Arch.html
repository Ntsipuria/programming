Arch Bridge

Arch bridges rely on a curved structure to distribute loads evenly, creating compressive forces that push outward along the curve into abutments.

Suitability

Areas with strong foundations for abutments.

Valleys, rivers, and areas needing long-lasting structures.

Suitable for both small and long spans.

Pros and Cons

Pros

High durability and strength.

Efficient in transferring loads.

Aesthetically pleasing.

Cons

Requires strong abutments.

Construction can be complex and costly.

Horizontal thrust needs to be managed.

Examples

Pont du Gard, France: A Roman aqueduct and engineering marvel.

Sydney Harbour Bridge, Australia: Steel through arch and a global icon.

Hell Gate Bridge, USA: A railway bridge known for its robust design.
