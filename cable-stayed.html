Cable-Stayed Bridge

A cable-stayed bridge has a deck supported directly by cables connected to towers. The cables can be arranged in a fan or harp pattern.

Suitability

Medium to long spans.

Urban areas needing sleek, modern designs.

Environments where lightweight construction is beneficial.

Pros and Cons

Pros

Requires fewer cables than suspension bridges.

Economical for medium spans.

Attractive, modern aesthetic.

Cons

Less suitable for extremely long spans.

Complex design and construction.

Maintenance of cables can be challenging.

Examples

Millau Viaduct, France: A record-breaking cable-stayed bridge.

Russky Bridge, Russia: The longest cable-stayed span in the world.

Sutong Yangtze River Bridge, China: A notable modern design.
