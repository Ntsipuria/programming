Beam Bridge

A beam bridge is the simplest type of bridge, consisting of a horizontal beam supported at both ends by piers. The load is directly transferred to the supports, making it straightforward to construct.

Suitability

Short spans (< 250 feet).

Flat terrains with stable support structures.

Budget-friendly construction.

Pros and Cons

Pros

Easy and quick to construct.

Cost-effective for short distances.

Minimal maintenance required.

Cons

Limited span length.

Aesthetically plain.

Vulnerable to strong winds and seismic forces.

Examples

Golden Gate Park Bridge, USA: Pedestrian-friendly beam bridge.

Donghai Bridge, China: Connects Shanghai to the Yangshan Deep-Water Port.
