Truss Bridge

Truss bridges are composed of connected triangular units that efficiently distribute loads, known for their strength and durability.

Suitability

Ideal for railway and road bridges.

Suitable for areas with heavy loads.

Can be used in rugged terrains.

Pros and Cons

Pros

High strength-to-weight ratio.

Economical for long spans.

Durable under heavy loads.

Cons

Requires regular maintenance.

Not as aesthetically appealing.

Can be costly for short spans.

Examples

Forth Bridge, Scotland: A UNESCO World Heritage site.

Quechee Gorge Bridge, USA: A Pratt truss design.

I-35W Saint Anthony Falls Bridge, USA: A modern truss bridge.
