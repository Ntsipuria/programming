Viaduct Bridge

Viaducts are long bridge structures consisting of a series of small spans, typically supported by piers.

Suitability

Crossing wide valleys or rugged terrain.

Suitable for railways and high-speed transportation.

Effective in urban areas needing elevated pathways.

Pros and Cons

Pros

Spans large distances with minimal interruption.

Durable and long-lasting.

Aesthetic in urban environments.

Cons

High construction costs.

Maintenance of multiple piers.

Examples

Glenfinnan Viaduct, Scotland: A famous curved railway viaduct.

Millau Viaduct, France: The tallest viaduct in the world.

Ponte Vecchio Viaduct, Italy: An ancient Roman viaduct.
